Sylabus of the subject

Learning outcomes of the course unit:

XML and related technologies are the main focus of this course. Students in the subject pass all stages of the life cycle of the document and its transformations and development with an emphasis on modern markup languages. Maintaining the appearance and layout of a document regardless of the destination format and presentation environment. Students will gain practical skills in the transformation of documents and the preparation of documents in the shape of the target output format. Subject introduces students to the basics of computer typesetting and typography recoverable even for traditional paper publishing.

Course contents:

  • Technology of the document creation and maintenance life cycle of. - Structure of the document: the logical and visual.
  • The life cycle of a document with an emphasis on the use of the Web. Representation and content management. The current site issues from the perspective of data.
  • Markup languages, their grammar, and the possibility of DTD validation of documents.
  • XML markup language, principles and applications.
  • Principles of XML transformations, related techniques and tools, input and output formats.
  • Program of processing, transformation and presentation of XML documents. The transformation language XSLT documents. Other languages, such as XPath, XML Schema, XQuery, and their use.
  • XML as a tool for web applications, verification and validation of XML documents used in Web services.
  • XML and database query and possibilities, in the future.
  • Cascading Style Sheets and their use for formatting Web documents.
  • DocBook style and its use. Editing options, and extension of the standard styles.
  • Universal desktop publishing and typography, the principles and terminology. Principles of web accessibility. Web standards, their distribution and development.
  • Content management systems: basic principles, possibilities and categorization.
